        <h3>What is it with the sky, you mean?</h3>
        <p>Sky, the source of mankind's higher power since antiquity. Due to its vastness and seemingly unending border, sky have represented the seat of higher being, as well as symbolizing hope for humankind perhaps since we have developed our beliefs.</p>
        <p>If you want to know what I mean, try looking up into the sky on the sunny morning (not the sun), perhaps you can feel peace radiating through you. The clouds drifting slowly with the wind. The blue that spread upward into the infinite space far beyond. That's where you feel you have become mere molecules, drifting along the clouds, dispersed into the air...</p>
          <p>On the other hand, sky is also a fascination in the scientific world, being a canvas for various objects and phenomenon of Earth's atmosphere and beyond, day and night--comets, planets, that shooting star you once made a wish with (has that wish ever come true?). Just think of the time you see the scattering of sunlight at dusk or dawn (the orange light touching you like fire, it asks about the fire inside of you), or the bright stars in the night (has they been gone already? Has your hope being hidden by those neon light?)</p>
        <p>And if the sky itself is not breathtaking enough for you, you can always pair it with the surface of the Earth to get more result, for example:</p>

      <section>
        <p id="ps">p.s. the sky is blue because the sunlight scatters (that is, light bounce off from molecules in the atmosphere) throughout the atmosphere. Due to physics, shorter wavelength like blue is better at scattering than longer wavelength like red or green (they become the white light). The same principle applies for sunset or sunrise. It just so happens that the atmosphere is thicker between you and the sun when it's near the horizon, so the blue is all scattered away and only more direct red or yellow appear.</p>
